Joshua Citarella - Career Timeline

A journey through the career of a contemporary artist and internet culture analyst

Early Career - The Jogging (2009)

In 2009, at 22 years old, Joshua Citarella started The Jogging, a popular net art blog site and originator of odd viral pictures circulating in the early 2010s.

Visit The Jogging

Higher Pictures (2013)

In 2013, Citarella's solo debut at Higher Pictures marked his entrance into the art world, at the age of 26. He displayed five large, crisp C-prints that exemplified his focus on digital manipulation.

Politigram and the Post-Left (2018)

In 2018, Citarella published "Politigram and the Post-Left", a key work that reflected his shift towards analyzing internet culture and political movements online.

Twitch Stream (2019-Present)

Citarella began streaming on Twitch in 2019, engaging directly with his audience while discussing topics like internet culture and contemporary art.

Left Futures Exhibition (2021)

In 2021, Citarella presented the "Left Futures" exhibition at Bas Fisher Invitational in Miami, continuing his exploration of internet culture and political futures.

20 Interviews Publication (2021)

Citarella published "20 Interviews" in 2021, which focuses on conversations about internet culture, politics, and art.

The Jogging and Net Art

Prior to these paintings in 2009 at 22 years old Joshua Citarella started The Jogging alongside fellow artist Brad Troemel; a popular net art blog site and originator of odd viral pictures circulating in the early 2010s.

https://www.tumblr.com/thejogging

Brad troemel speaks on the thinking behind The Jogging in his essay "The Accidental Audience," published in The New Inquiry on March 14, 2013 .

"Jogging began as a Tumblr in 2009 that displayed Google Image Searched products Photoshopped together as artworks, with the same text you'd find on any museum wall placard: title, date, medium, and author. Discrepancies would arise between the visual content and the descriptions, as digital collages were variously titled as installations, paintings, or sculptures, raising questions about the leveling effects of installation photography on all mediums and the power that documentary images have over the physical objects they depict."

Virality

The virality / popularity of The Jogging played a crucial role in Joshua and Brads career trajectory establishing themselves in the art world through Tumblr. This stands as a point of inspiration. There is a need for digital performance as an artist; that the inst posts, tags, comments, shares etc. are a part of the performance and not just that but can lead to a beneficial outcome where in this case it poured jet fuel onto their careers.

I could go through and analyse each of his works and how they link together however I don't believe that to be the purpose of this research.

There is a key ideology underpinning joshuas ability to move across digital space, performing an identity and exploring interesting ideas, creating, conducting research along the way. As for the practice of making this ideology is somewhat best reflected in this quote from 2013.

"The contemporary means of image production unprecedentedly lend themselves to the transmutation of signs. RGB pixels are implicated as new prima material delivering objects from the dim confines of materiality into omnipresent electronic radiance. A global network of images has transfigured all bodies and materials into exchanges of energy. The world is now transcribed through photography in order to be reconfigured with graphics-editing software and transformed into its ideal state. While this universal translation space now allows for an alchemical magnum opus, it has consequently democratized all materials transmuting lead into gold and vice versa. We lose sight of the interface and are carried away in the fervor of a transcendent promise fulfilled.' - Joshua Citarella" (christopherschreck, 2013)

Reflections

The underlying idea that can be extracted from this is that the transmutability of the image, objects, sculptures through 3d space, photoshop etc can be a means of breaking out of restrictions whether of showcasing works irl; ie photoshoping them into a gallery space or physically by utilising print or photoshop to make use of materials that would cost money irl.

This mentality paired with the mentality of performing online as an artist that brad troemel goes into is one of the key aspects of Joshua Citarellas practice that I hope to integrate into my own.
